## The Thinking Game

**Summary:**  
This article discusses how DeepMind and its founder Demis Hassabis revolutionized through artificial intelligence technology, from game development to solving fundamental scientific challenges (such as protein folding). Hassabis initially gained fame as a chess prodigy but eventually shifted toward AI research, founding DeepMind with the goal of "solving intelligence" and applying it to complex problems. Through projects like AlphaGo, AlphaZero, and AlphaStar, DeepMind demonstrated AI breakthroughs in gaming, strategy, and scientific prediction, particularly AlphaFold's achievement in protein folding, which solved a 50-year biology puzzle and earned the Nobel Prize. The article also explores the ethical risks of AGI (Artificial General Intelligence), such as technological loss of control and militarization, while emphasizing DeepMind's balance between technological advancement and safety ethics. Finally, it uses the art installation *The Creation of Adam* as a metaphor for the symbiotic relationship between humans and AI, implying that AGI will fundamentally transform the course of human civilization.

---

**Key Points:**  
- **Demis Hassabis' Background**: Initially renowned as a chess prodigy but realized chess was not his true mission, leading him to AI research.  
- **Founding of DeepMind**: Established with Shane Legg and Mustafa Suleyman, aiming to "solve intelligence" and apply it across all fields.  
- **Key AI Projects**:  
  - **AlphaGo**: Defeated human Go champion Lee Sedol in 2016, proving AI's capability in complex games.  
  - **AlphaZero**: Achieved superiority over human expertise without learning human chess strategies from scratch.  
  - **AlphaStar**: Demonstrated multi-line operations and tactical deception in *StarCraft II*.  
- **Breakthrough in Protein Folding**:  
  - **AlphaFold**: Solved the 50-year-old puzzle with a 92.4 score at CASP14 in 2020, open-sourced code, and released a database of 200 million protein structures, accelerating drug development and environmental governance.  
  - **Nobel Prize**: Hassabis and John Jumper received the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for AlphaFold.  
- **Ethics and Risks**:  
  - AGI could be used for autonomous weapons, surveillance, or舆论 manipulation, raising ethical dilemmas similar to the Manhattan Project.  
  - DeepMind emphasizes responsible AI, balancing technological acceleration with safety ethics.  
- **Cultural Metaphor**:  
  - *The Creation of Adam* art installation symbolizes the symbiotic relationship between AI and humans, suggesting AGI will divide human civilization into "pre-AI" and "post-AI" eras.

## Why can't I remember what I've read?

**Summary in English:**  
This article explores the dilemma modern people face with information overload—despite collecting numerous tutorials and tools, it is difficult to truly remember and apply the learned content. The author introduces the core argument through the YouTube video "How to Remember Everything You Read": the key to memory lies not in storing information, but in digestion and processing. The video creator Justin Song proposes the PACER system, which categorizes information into five types (procedural, analogical, conceptual, evidential, and referential), and provides targeted digestion methods for each category, such as practice, critical analysis, schematic understanding, extracting conclusions, and organizing collections. The article emphasizes that in the AI era, humans should shift from "storing information" to "processing information," enhancing cognitive efficiency through systematic methods rather than blindly pursuing memory volume.

---

**Key Points in Bullet Points:**  
- **Information Overload and Memory Challenges**: Modern people collect numerous tutorials and tools but struggle to apply them due to a lack of digestion, leading to the illusion of "collecting equals owning."  
- **Introduction of the PACER System**: Justin Song emphasizes through his video that memory's core is information processing rather than mere storage, proposing a five-category classification of information and digestion strategies.  
- **Digestion Methods for Five Types of Information**:  
  - **Procedural Information (P)**: Digest through practice (e.g., operating AI tools) rather than rote memorization.  
  - **Analogical Information (A)**: Critically analyze analogies (e.g., comparing AI's internet connectivity to an intern), understanding similarities and differences.  
  - **Conceptual Information (C)**: Reconstruct knowledge networks using schematics (mind maps), grasping abstract principles (e.g., the formation mechanism of AI hallucination).  
  - **Evidential Information (E)**: Extract conclusions rather than relying on specific data (e.g., focusing on the core conclusions of model performance rather than scores).  
  - **Referential Information (R)**: Organize collections (e.g., bookmarks, notes), building a rapid retrieval system instead of memorizing trivial details.  
- **Cognitive Upgrade in the AI Era**: Emphasizes shifting from "information consumption" to "information digestion," enhancing knowledge retention and application through systematic methods rather than pursuing information volume.  
- **Case Studies and Criticism**: Uses the movie *Rain Man* (Kim Peek) as an extreme case of "memory ability," criticizing the limitations of pure memorization and advocating for deepening understanding through logical connections and critical thinking.  

---

**References Mentioned:**  
1. **YouTube Video**: *How to Remember Everything You Read* (Justin Song's video, core theoretical source).  
2. **Movie *Rain Man* (Rain Man)**: As a reference for the extreme case of "memory ability," criticizing the limitations of pure memorization.  
3. **AI Tools and Cases**: Such as ChatGPT, DeepSeek V3.2, Sora-generated videos, as examples for information classification and digestion.  

---  

**Note:** The article addresses information overload through systematic methods (PACER), emphasizing "processing information" rather than "storing information," applicable to learning and cognitive optimization in the AI era.

#### Translation 

**Summary in Chinese:**  
文章探讨了现代人面对信息过载时的困境——尽管收藏了大量教程和工具，却难以真正记住和应用所学内容。作者通过YouTube视频《How to Remember Everything You Read》引出核心观点：记忆的关键不在于存储信息，而在于消化与处理。视频创作者贾斯汀・宋提出PACER系统，将信息分为五类（程序性、类比性、概念性、证据性、参考性），并为每类信息提供针对性的消化方法，如实践、批判性分析、构图理解、提取结论和整理收藏。文章强调，在AI时代，人类应从“存储信息”转向“处理信息”，通过系统化方法提升认知效率，而非盲目追求记忆量。

**Key Points in Bullet Points:**  
- **信息过载与记忆困境**：现代人收藏大量教程和工具，却因缺乏消化而难以应用，导致“收藏即拥有”的错觉。  
- **PACER系统的提出**：贾斯汀・宋通过视频强调，记忆的核心是信息处理而非单纯存储，提出五类信息的分类及消化策略。  
- **五类信息的消化方法**：  
  - **程序性信息（P）**：通过实践（如操作AI工具）消化，而非死记硬背。  
  - **类比性信息（A）**：批判性分析类比（如将AI联网功能比作实习生），理解其相似点与差异。  
  - **概念性信息（C）**：用构图（思维导图）重构知识网络，理解抽象原理（如AI幻觉的形成机制）。  
  - **证据性信息（E）**：提取结论而非依赖具体数据（如关注模型性能的核心结论而非分数）。  
  - **参考性信息（R）**：整理收藏（如书签、备忘录），建立快速检索系统，而非记忆琐碎细节。  
- **AI时代的认知升级**：强调从“信息消费”转向“信息消化”，通过系统化方法提升知识留存率与应用能力，而非追求信息量。  
- **案例与批判**：以电影《雨人》（Kim Peek）为例，批判单纯记忆的局限性，倡导通过逻辑关联和批判思维深化理解。

## STATE OF AI IN BUSINESS 2025

### Core Interpretation and Key Strategies of the 2025 Commercial AI Current Status Report

#### I. Current Status and Challenges of the Generative AI Gap  
1. **Gap Between Technology and Application**  
   - Enterprises generally face the "high adoption, low transformation" dilemma: advanced technical parameters but difficulties in practical implementation, AI tools disconnected from business processes.  
   - **Key Issues**: AI tools cannot continuously learn, fail to adapt to specific workflows, lack context memory capabilities, leading to employee resistance in adoption.  

2. **Industry Differences**  
   - **High Adoption Industries** (e.g., sales, marketing): High budget investment, but limited actual effectiveness, relying on external outsourcing.  
   - **Low Transformation Industries** (e.g., legal, procurement): Though not widely adopted, AI can significantly reduce costs (e.g., saving $10 million to $100 million annually).  

3. **Data and Time Costs**  
   - Enterprises take an average of 18 months from demand to implementation, and switching costs are extremely high once integrated with AI tools (e.g., contract templates, approval rules need retraining).  

---

#### II. Three Core Strategies to Cross the AI Gap  

**1. Prioritize Purchasing Over In-House Development**  
- **Reason**: Success rate of in-house AI projects is 33%, while external collaboration projects have a 67% implementation rate.  
- **Key Selection Criteria**:  
  - **Customization Capability**: Suppliers must adapt to enterprise workflows (e.g., approval processes, data formats).  
  - **Continuous Learning**: Tools must improve based on feedback (e.g., model iteration).  
- **Case**: A financial company completed a risk assessment AI pilot in 2 months via an external supplier, saving $1 million annually in outsourcing costs.  

**2. Let Frontline Teams Lead AI Selection**  
- **Core Logic**:  
  - **Frontline Teams** (e.g., procurement managers, super users) better understand pain points and can rapidly validate tool effectiveness.  
  - **Central Labs** often select "showy" tools, but employee adoption rates are low.  
- **Data Support**: Medium-sized enterprises can scale from pilot to full deployment in 90 days, while large enterprises with central leadership take over 9 months.  
- **Practical Recommendations**:  
  - Let frontline managers lead selection, pilot first, then report to headquarters;  
  - Prioritize testing "super users" (e.g., employees frequently using ChatGPT).  

**3. Focus on Learning Ability, Not Technical Parameters**  
- **Core Metrics**:  
  - **Context Memory** (63% of executives care): Tools must remember historical dialogues/data.  
  - **Feedback Improvement** (66% care): Models must adjust based on user feedback.  
  - **Workflow Adaptation**: Tools must seamlessly integrate with existing enterprise processes.  
- **Avoid Pitfalls**:  
  - Avoid purchasing generic tools (e.g., only capable of content generation but unable to learn);  
  - Choose AI with continuous memory functions (e.g., Microsoft, OpenAI's latest tools).  

---

#### III. Future Trends: Agentic Web  

1. **Technological Evolution Direction**  
   - **From Single Tools to Agent Collaboration**: Future AI will form an "Agent Network," enabling cross-platform, cross-organization collaboration (e.g., procurement agents autonomously negotiate, customer service agents communicate across channels).  
   - **Key Protocols**: Like MCP, A2A, similar to "AI internet protocols," enabling interoperability between enterprise tools.  

2. **Enterprise Competitive Barriers**  
   - **First-Mover Advantage**: The next 18 months is a critical window; enterprises must pre-emptively adopt AI tools with learning capabilities, or risk being locked into giant ecosystems.  
   - **Case**: Microsoft and OpenAI have already embedded continuous memory functions; delayed adoption will lead to high switching costs.  

---

#### IV. Action Recommendations and Summary  

**1. Take Immediate Action**  
- **Window Period**: The next 18 months is the final opportunity to cross the AI gap; enterprises must make quick decisions.  
- **Priorities**:  
  - Choose external suppliers with learning capabilities;  
  - Let frontline teams lead selection and pilot;  
  - Focus on backend departments (e.g., legal, procurement) automation potential.  

**2. Avoid Common Pitfalls**  
- **Pitfall 1**: Blindly pursue technical parameters (e.g., model size), ignoring actual business adaptability.  
- **Pitfall 2**: Rely on IT departments to work in isolation, neglecting frontline needs.  
- **Pitfall 3**: Focus only on front-end sales/marketing, ignoring backend cost optimization.  

**3. Core Conclusion**  
- **The Generative AI Gap Is Not Permanent**, but enterprises must make differentiated choices:  
  - **Choose tools that understand your business** (not just more expensive models);  
  - **Rely on frontline teams** (not technical teams) to drive transformation;  
  - **Solve real problems** (not chase trends).  

**Final Goal**: Use AI to reduce external expenses (e.g., outsourcing, consulting), liberate employee productivity, and achieve a leap from "repetitive labor" to "value creation."

#### Translation 

### 《2025年商业AI现状报告》核心解读与关键策略

#### 一、生成式AI鸿沟的现状与挑战
1. **技术与应用的差距**  
   - 企业普遍面临“高采用、低转型”的困境：技术参数先进但实际落地困难，AI工具与业务流程脱节。
   - **关键问题**：AI工具无法持续学习、无法适配具体工作流、缺乏上下文记忆能力，导致员工抵触使用。

2. **行业差异**  
   - **高采用行业**（如销售、营销）：预算投入大，但实际效果有限，依赖外部外包。
   - **低转型行业**（如法务、采购）：虽未大规模应用，但AI能显著降低成本（如节省100-1000万美元/年）。

3. **数据与时间成本**  
   - 企业从需求到落地平均耗时18个月，且一旦对接AI工具，切换成本极高（如合同模板、审批规则需重新训练）。

---

#### 二、跨过AI鸿沟的三大核心策略

**1. 优先购买而非自建**  
- **原因**：自建AI项目成功率为33%，而外部合作项目落地率高达67%。  
- **关键选择**：  
  - **定制化能力**：供应商需能适配企业工作流（如审批流程、数据格式）。  
  - **持续学习**：工具需具备从反馈中改进的能力（如模型迭代）。  
- **案例**：某金融企业通过外部供应商2个月完成风险评估AI试点，年省100万美元外包成本。

**2. 由业务前线主导AI选型**  
- **核心逻辑**：  
  - **前线团队**（如采购经理、超级用户）更了解痛点，能快速验证工具有效性。  
  - **中央实验室**常选“炫技”工具，但员工实际使用率低。  
- **数据支持**：中型企业试点到全量部署仅需90天，而大企业中央主导需9个月以上。  
- **实践建议**：  
  - 让一线经理主导选型，试点后上报总部；  
  - 优先测试已使用个人AI工具的“超级用户”（如常使用ChatGPT的员工）。

**3. 关注学习能力，而非技术参数**  
- **核心指标**：  
  - **上下文记忆**（63%高管关注）：工具需记住历史对话/数据。  
  - **反馈改进**（66%关注）：模型需根据用户反馈调整。  
  - **工作流适配**：工具需无缝集成企业现有流程。  
- **避坑指南**：  
  - 避免购买通用工具（如仅能生成内容但无法学习）；  
  - 选择具备持续记忆功能的AI（如微软、OpenAI最新工具）。

---

#### 三、未来趋势：智能体网络（Agentic Web）

1. **技术演进方向**  
   - **从单体工具到智能体协作**：未来AI将形成“智能体网络”，跨平台、跨组织协作（如采购智能体自主谈判、客户服务智能体跨渠道沟通）。  
   - **关键协议**：如MCP、A2A等，类似“AI互联网协议”，实现不同企业工具的互联互通。

2. **企业竞争壁垒**  
   - **先发优势**：未来18个月是关键窗口期，企业需提前布局具备学习能力的AI工具，否则将被巨头生态锁定。  
   - **案例**：微软、OpenAI已开始嵌入持续记忆功能，企业若延迟布局，将面临高切换成本。

---

#### 四、行动建议与总结

**1. 立即行动**  
- **窗口期**：未来18个月是跨过AI鸿沟的最后机会，企业需快速决策。  
- **优先级**：  
  - 选择具备学习能力的外部供应商；  
  - 让前线团队主导选型与试点；  
  - 重点关注后台部门（如法务、采购）的自动化潜力。

**2. 避免常见误区**  
- **误区1**：盲目追求技术参数（如模型参数量），忽视实际业务适配性。  
- **误区2**：依赖IT部门闭门造车，忽略一线需求。  
- **误区3**：仅关注前端销售/营销，忽视后台成本优化。

**3. 核心结论**  
- **生成式AI鸿沟并非永久**，但需企业做出差异化选择：  
  - **选更懂你的工具**（而非更贵的模型）；  
  - **靠前线团队**（而非技术团队）推动转型；  
  - **解决真问题**（而非追风口）。  

**最终目标**：通过AI减少外部支出（如外包、咨询），解放员工生产力，实现从“重复劳动”到“价值创造”的跃迁。
